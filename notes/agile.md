# The Death of Agile by Allen Holub

## The Wrong Questions:
- What is the role of the project manager? - There is NO project manager in an Agile team.
- What is the process we have to follow? - Agile is not a canned process, you cannot purchase and apply it. 

## What Is Agile - Really
- The way that people work with one another is more important than processes. This does not say instead of. Processes are important but the processes are not canned things. Individuals come up with processes as they interact with each other.
- Scrum != Agile. Scrum is a process, Agile is not.
- Test - In any sentence that has Agile in it, if you can replace the word Agile with flexible, then the sentence is correct, otherwise, it's nonsense.
- Agile is an organizational thing. The organization as a whole has to be agile, not the teams. Whatever you need now, you should be able to get it now. 
- No such thing as deadlines in the Agile world. Everything is based on priorities and not deadlines.
- But deadlines are real, how do we manage that? deadlines are of course real things, and to deal with them, all you can is reduce scope. You observe the process and make a projection based on your observation. You cannot estimate things in the traditional sense and no milestones in the traditional sense. 
- The key is to reduce everything that adds friction between the team and adding value to the customer(s).
- How to do scale Agile? Look at how Spotify organized its teams. No hierarchy. No org chart in a tree form. We have a matrix. No boss. We have individual tribes. There are small teams of around 8 and a single system architect type who exists among the teams. No one orders anyone around - this is a collaborative process

## What is Scrum
- Scrum is NOT Agile.
- Scrum is a small thing, Agile is a big thing. Scrum is defined by a 16 page document, Agile is a set of techniques to be more flexible.
- Scrum has stolen a few ideas from XP and not the full thing. Stand ups and planning sessions are XP ideas. 
- The idea in XP is to have your customer, your actual customer in the room with you. Scrum has replaced this with a Product Owner, who is often not the customer but a rep of the marketing department. 
- Scrum has this idea of sizing stories to fit in this concept of a sprint. This flies in the face of agility. 
- Certificates are snake oil. 
- The worst case of this is SAFe. 
- SAFe is designed to be acceptable to corporations. SAFe is the same old stuff companies used to do with scrum teams jammed at the bottom of the hierarchy.
- Mixed into this is a set of tool vendors like JIRA that force waterfall style management.

## So Again - What Is Agile
### CULTURE
- Agile is culture not a process. Agile is the attitudes and behaviors of a particular social group.
### TRUST
- You don't have managers because you trust people to do their work.
### COMMUNICATION, SIMPLICITY, COURAGE, FEEDBACK
- This is the culture.
- The process itself is very simple. You start by talking to the customer and figuring out what they need. Then you break off a chunk of that, implement it, and give it to them. You do this is a short, 2ish week loop and continue.
- You don't need any tools for this. You don't need JIRA to have a 3 million story backlog.

## Main Goal - Produce Working Software
- If nobody is gonna read it, don't write documentation for it, it's waste. Documentation is all paperwork, not just software doc.
- Don't negotiate with your customer, collaborate with them. This is beyond contract, this is day to day work.
- Retros are dumb - If you have to retro on something that was bad over 3 weeks, you are not agile. True agile means you can adapt on the spot, make quick changes on the spot.
- Your job is not to do cool things, your job is to add value to your customer. You are in a service position, you're not doing cool techy stuff to make you fee good. 
- Reqs will always change and so will the work. You have to be able to accept reqs changing at any time, no matter how late into the process.

## Individuals Are More Important Than Anything
- In good teams, individuals should not have to argue to be able to do their jobs. You cannot be agile in this scenario. 
- Teams have to organize themselves. You can't assign people to do jobs, teams have to form naturally and come together naturally.
